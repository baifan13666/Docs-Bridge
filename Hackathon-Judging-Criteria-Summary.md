Hackathon Judging Criteria Summary

The evaluation is divided into three main categories:

Technical

Business

Pitch

Each subcategory has 10% weightage, meaning every item contributes equally to the total score.

1. Preliminary Round (Focus: AI Development & Implementation)

The preliminary round mainly evaluates how well the AI system is built and integrated.

Technical (50%)
1. AI Implementation Strategy & Configuration

Demonstrates strong understanding of the assigned AI technology.

Uses advanced configurations and best practices.

The AI solution is properly customized for the problem.

In simple terms:
How well your team applies AI technology.

2. Data Strategy & Engineering

Data sources are relevant and well chosen.

Proper data preprocessing, cleaning, and augmentation are performed.

Handles noise, bias, or limited data effectively.

In simple terms:
How strong your data pipeline and data quality management are.

3. Model Performance & Validation

Uses appropriate evaluation metrics.

Demonstrates clear model performance results.

Includes train/test splits and performance analysis.

In simple terms:
Whether the AI model actually works and is properly validated.

4. System Integration

AI is integrated into the application pipeline.

The system handles automatic input/output.

Not just isolated scripts or manual execution.

In simple terms:
Whether the AI is part of a real working system.

5. Technical Feasibility & Scalability

Code is modular and well structured.

Designed with efficiency and scalability in mind.

Feasible for real-world deployment.

In simple terms:
Whether the project could be deployed beyond a prototype.

Business (40%)
1. Market Potential & Demand

Solves a real and significant problem.

Has a clear target audience.

Shows scalability and adoption potential.

Key idea:
Is there real demand for this solution?

2. Impact & Social Value

Creates meaningful positive impact.

Supports inclusivity or sustainability goals.

Key idea:
Does the project benefit society?

3. Sustainability

Shows long-term maintenance strategy.

AI models can be updated and retrained over time.

Key idea:
Can the solution continue to operate in the future?

4. Innovation & Creativity

Introduces new ideas or approaches.

Clearly different from existing solutions.

Key idea:
How creative and original the solution is.

2. Final Round (Focus: Product Readiness & Impact)

The final round focuses more on product quality, usability, and long-term potential.

Technical (50%)
1. AI Innovation & Technical Depth

Demonstrates advanced technical architecture.

Uses AI in a highly innovative way.

Focus: Depth and originality of the AI technology.

2. System Performance & Latency

System runs smoothly with low latency.

Supports real-time interactions.

Focus: System speed, efficiency, and responsiveness.

3. Continued Development Possibilities

Clear future roadmap.

System is scalable and expandable.

Focus: Long-term product evolution potential.

4. Inclusivity & Accessibility (UI/UX)

Interface is user-friendly and inclusive.

Follows universal design principles.

Focus: Accessibility for all types of users.

5. Data Integrity & Ethics

Protects data privacy and security.

AI models are transparent and fair.

Addresses bias and ethical concerns.

Focus: Responsible and ethical AI use.

Business (40%)
1. Market Potential & Demand

Strong market relevance and scalability.

2. Sustainability

Long-term technical and operational viability.

3. Competitive Advantages

Clearly defined unique selling points (USP).

Demonstrates advantages over existing competitors.

4. Social Impact

Creates measurable positive change.

Improves access to services or supports underserved communities.
